Liam Cashel

Manager, Product Management

Liam specializes in leading consumer products from concept-to-launch for a variety of clients in the financial services industry. Liam believes that excellent PMs must pair strong product sense with technical fluency. To that end Liam completed MITs 32-week intensive full stack coding bootcamp to build on his existing technical knowledge earned through a dual degree in Mathematics and Finance from the College of William and Mary.

Product Experience

Throughout his career Liam has developed a strong familiarity with the payment and money movement space. Payments are an interesting challenge for a PM. On top of the traditional juggling act of user needs, technical feasibility and development time, payments add the additional complexity of stringent regulatory requirements. Liam thoroughly enjoys the difficulty of balancing these four needs to deliver exceptional user experiences.

Lending Origination

Currently Liam is supporting a regional bank’s sixty million dollar effort to fully overhaul the front and back end of their lending originations platform. Liam leverages his subject matter expertise to provide strategic advise to the banks technology leaders. Working closely with client leadership, Liam has defined the bank’s product roadmap, rollout strategy, MVP, regulatory requirements, and communications strategy. In addition to strategic support, Liam also provides operational support by being the Product Owner for Lending Product Pricing, Unsecured Installment Loans, Communications and Business Card.

Zelle Payments

In his previous role Liam served as the Zelle Payments Product Manager at one of the countries largest consumer banks, where their 23+ million users moved billions of dollars via Zelle annually. Liam increased new user engagement by 35% by rebuilding Zelle Limits to better suit the client’s newly enrolled users’ needs. Additionally, Liam discovered an authentication vulnerability in the customer enrollment process with the potential to lead to serious cases of fraud. Taking the initiative, Liam proposed, then led the research, design & rollout of a new mandatory OTP verification flow for 22 million users.

  • Zelle's Homepage

Personal Loan Payments

Liam served as the Product Owner for Personal Loan Payments. Liam led the product from concept-to-launch and enabled hundreds of thousands of customers to seamless pay tens of millions of dollars worth of personal loans. Liam utilized the bank’s existing ACH transfer rail architecture and adapted it to both his users needs and the regulatory requirements inherent to payments. Liam collaborated with other Product teams as well as a variety of external teams including: Design, UX Research, Development, Legal, and Retail. Liam successfully launched the platform and in doing so reduced payment processing time from 6 days to 1 day, drastically cut the clients costs by eliminating a third-party system, and helped increase personal loan enrollment by 30%.

Gavel

Winning solution of the 2019 Idealab case study challenge

The vast majority of Gen Z use some sort of P2P payment solution on their phones. Gavel fills a currently unoccupied niche within this space. What can be dubbed the P2S, Peer to Stranger, space. With a conventional P2P payment platform once money is exchanged it is difficult to ensure both parties live up to the terms of their agreement. Gavel combined 3 concepts to drastically reduce this transaction risk.
